Title:
Arithmetic Sector Bifurcation in Quadratic Continued Fractions: A Formalized Study of the Wallis--PCF Family

Authors:
Papanokechi

Primary MSC:
11A55

Secondary MSC:

Keywords:
polynomial continued fractions, generalized continued fractions, hypergeometric functions, Clausen identity, modular forms, Gamma function, irrationality, PSLQ.

Abstract:
We develop a two-level classification of polynomial continued fractions by a universal Archimedean transfer law and a local arithmetic signature (Conjecture B). This survey develops that proposal across five recent manuscripts. Ratio universality for Meinardus-class partition functions first identifies a macro-spectral law whose leading coefficient is independent of family-specific data. Arithmetic topology then shows that this law is degenerate as a classifier: families with identical coarse invariants can occupy distinct arithmetic sectors. Our main synthesis theorem is a three-sector decomposition of the PCF landscape into logarithmic families, Wallis-Gamma/pi families, and a V-Quad sector detected by congruence obstruction and weak lifting stability. The formal backbone is provided by the WallisFamily Lean 4 package (modules ShiftConsistency and CasoratianPi4), which verifies the shift operator transport and Casoratian bridge with no sorry placeholders. Borel regularization and Stokes-connection diagnostics suggest that the V-Quad archipelago marks the boundary of classical closed-form behavior.
